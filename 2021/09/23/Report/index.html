Recommander System Report
emo | 2021年09月23日 | bigdata

存档。课上要求写的报告作业。

Abstract

In today’s information explosion, the massive, rapid, diversified, and valuable data makes big
data come into being. Daily life has long been inseparable from all kinds of Internet
applications. One of the most familiar technologies is the intelligent recommender system. This
report will focus on the intelligent recommender system and talk about the specific application
scenarios of the recommender system, the embodiment of big data characteristics, related
problems, and their solutions.

1 Application Domain

1.1 What is the recommender system

A recommender system is a tool that automatically contacts users and items. It collects users’
information and analyzes users’ interests and hobbies to make personalized
recommendations. As Steve Jobs said: “ People don’t know what they want until you show it
to them.” This kind of recommendation can be done without giving out keywords describing
their needs, and it can proactively recommend customized information that may meet their
interests and needs.

So what are the benefits of a recommender system? Imagine that Wal-Mart, which we are
familiar with, often places popular goods in the most eye-catching position - the reason is
obvious. They are popular and can bring higher income to the supermarket. However, after
all, these popular goods account for only a few, and the demand for most of the goods in
inventory is relatively low. If we try to sell these commodities with low demand, we are
likely to make more profits than popular mainstream commodities. This idea comes from the
interesting The Long Tail theory. By analogy, we can expand commodities into information
and data on the network (including commodities in online shopping malls, multimedia
content such as video and music, advertisements on Web pages, etc.). No matter what kind of
information, as long as it exists, someone may need it, even if only one person on earth needs
it. At this time, the benefits of the recommender system are very obvious. We can
recommend thousands of information to the suitable people through intelligent
recommendation technology. In this way, with the flow of information, both producers and
consumers can get what they want. It overcomes the problem of information overload,
provides customized recommendations, and improves the efficiency of information
dissemination. Therefore, the recommender system is undoubtedly a great invention that can
promote the progress of human society in the era of big data.

1.2 specific application recommender system

What specific cases currently have adopted such a recommender system? Almost every
website or web service we are familiar with will use recommendation technology. Here are
some specific application cases.

  • E -commerce scenario:

When we are shopping on Amazon, when browsing a certain product, we often see similar
products recommended by Amazon for us below, and modules such as “people who bought
this product also bought…”, which optimizes shopping Experience.

  • Media scenario:

when we open Youtube, the homepage will show videos recommended based on your
viewing history; when we visit Netflix, the Netflix recommendation system will help us
easily find our favorite movies or TVs program. There is also the popular Tiktok short video
app in recent years. As you slide up, it will continuously recommend short video content you
may like.

  • Social network scenario:

You must have seen the “Suggest Friends” module on social platforms such as Facebook and
Twitter. The recommendation system here will recommend people who may become friends
for users based on social graphs, interest graphs, etc.

  • advertising scenario:

Although many people do not like to see annoying ads when browsing the web but have to
admit: If we have to look at the ad, then they would like to see some related to their own,
their ads may be of interest. With recommendation technology, advertisements are like WalMart products and are accurately pushed to the right users. This is how Google Ads works.

In addition to the above, there are many scenarios where personalized recommendation
technology is used. It can be said that the recommendation system does provide people with
the possibility of finding a needle in a haystack in this era of massive data.

2 Look data from a recommender system 4 V’s

The reason why big data is known as “big”, is due to it has four Vs-volume, velocity, variety,
and veracity. In this chapter, we will interpret the recommendation system from these four
perspectives.

2.1 Volume

Volume refers to the total amount of data. There is no doubt that the background of the birth
of the recommendation system is that we now have a lot of data. On the Internet, every
operation (click the mouse, transaction, email, search…) of everyone is generating huge
amounts of data. It is precisely because there is too much information on the Internet that not
only producers are eager to let their information be seen, but consumers are also eager to find
the content they need most efficiently, and the recommender system came into being.

Moreover, the operation of the recommendation system is also inseparable from a large
amount of data. Take Amazon as an example, the recommendation system needs a lot of user
information, product information, and order information as “materials” before it can finally
analyze the recommendation results. Such analysis and statistics cannot be done based on a
small amount of data.

2.2 Variety

Variety means that there are many types of data. Big data is composed of a small part of
structured data and most unstructured data. It is most of the unstructured data that is closely
related to human information. Therefore, the recommendation system should be able to find
links and analyze key information from various forms of data (e-mail, web pages, social
records, music, pictures, videos…). This is closely related to deep learning, knowledge graph,
and other fields.

2.3 Velocity

Refers to the rapid generation of data for consumption. The recommender system will
respond to and deal with such fast-paced changes promptly, so the recommendation results
we see are also changing all the time. For example, on the homepage of news websites, hot
topics will be recommended for us. And this hot topic list is changing every day, even every
hour.

2.4 Veracity

Veracity refers to the deviation, forgery, and abnormal part of the data that should be
considered in the analysis of big data. There may be huge differences in the quality of data. In
the recommender system, measures are taken to prevent dirty data from damaging the
integrity and correctness of the data system, which is conducive to improving the relevance
and improving the accuracy of the recommendation.

3 Big Data face in Recommendation Systems Related Challenges

The characteristics of big data determine its important issues: data collection, data storage,
data processing, data mining, and data security.

3.1 collection issues

About collection, perhaps we can think of salespeople who distribute questionnaires on the
street. This most traditional way of filling out questionnaires is of course also a kind of data
collection, but it is no longer enough in today’s society. The era of big data emphasizes the
collection of complete sets of data, to provide more possibilities that humans cannot predict.

For the recommendation system, perhaps the biggest problem is that a large amount of data is
needed to produce recommendation results. This is why the most prominent recommendation
systems come from large companies that have more data, such as Google, Amazon, and
Netflix. How to collect the complete works and solve the “cold start” problem has become
one of the challenges.

3.2 storage problems

The proper storage of the collected data is undoubtedly the basis for the implementation of
the recommender system. So where should users, media, orders, goods, music… All this
provided by the platform be stored? Traditional storage methods can not accommodate, the
access speed is slow and the risk is also high. If the data is scattered, how to solve the
problem of data synchronization? These are all about storage.

3.3 data processing problems

Imagine that a large website needs to provide personalized recommendations for millions of
users at the same time. How to perform calculations? Although parallel computing is
currently an important means to solve a huge amount of calculations, there are still some
problems. For example, in parallel computing, there is no guarantee that the amount of
calculation for each small task is the same. As a result, the server that has completed its
calculation task needs to wait for individual servers that have not yet been completed. The
final calculation speed depends on the final completed subtask. The efficiency is reduced.

3.4 data mining question

How to dig out valuable information from a bunch of chaotic data is the key to machine
intelligence and the mission of big data. In the recommender system, this question can be
understood as: how to deal with polymorphic data? How to finally discover the information
that users may be interested in through various data? How to optimize the algorithm to
recommend more accuracy? How to avoid recommending popular objects? (Such as the
Harry Potter problem)

3.5 Data Security and Privacy

In big data, data security and personal privacy are classic topics. Business losses and users’
safety are hidden dangers behind technical candy. Take the recommender system for
example. Although it provides convenience for users most of the time, many people don’t like
the feeling of being monitored by the platform. For example, when you use your mobile
browser to browse the web and involve some goods, or even mention them in a chat with
your friends, you may see the recommendation of the goods on the shopping app the next
minute - whether you need it or not. Moreover, various applications also collect very detailed
user data and behaviors to analyze users’ preferences.

4 Solution

A complete recommendation system consists of four parts: the recording module for
collecting user behavior information, the model analysis module for analyzing and processing
user preferences, the recommendation algorithm module (core), and the feedback processing
module. This section will discuss solutions to the problems mentioned in Section 3 around
each module.

4.1 Data Collection

Take Facebook as an example. Facebook can generate more accurate and comprehensive
portraits by analyzing the active sharing and interaction of users. Facebook will analyze users ‘
information in 98 aspects to maximize the effect of advertising, such as the location of the
house, the wedding anniversary within 30 days, the industry they are engaged in, and the
possible move in the near future. In the personal profile, Facebook provides options for users
to share information such as hometown, current residence, graduate school, marital status, and
religious beliefs. This information allows Facebook to better analyze users’ daily life behavior
and locate users’ hobbies and value orientation, then customize push ads.

4.2 Data Distributed Storage

Large websites will first distribute servers around the world. In Amazon, for example, 1.5
billion product catalog data from retail stores can be disseminated and stored globally in S3
through 200 implementation centers, with nearly 500 million updates per week. At the same
time, product catalogs of data on the S3 interface are analyzed every 30 minutes and sent
back to different databases.

4.3 Cloud Computing

Cloud computing technology emerges for efficient processing. Cloud computing provides
scalable, inexpensive distributed computing power over the network. Users can access all
kinds of IT resources they need anytime, anywhere, and expand their large data applications
in the cloud through a variety of tools, such as data storage, data collection, data location,
Management, data sharing, and data collaboration. For example, Amazon’s flexible
MapReduce program is built on top of the Hadoop framework and complements each other to
help retailers efficiently manage and leverage the analysis platform.

4.4 Data Mining

How to dig out the real preferences of users? As the earliest and most well-known
recommendation algorithm, collaborative filtering has not only been in-depth research in
academia, but it is still widely used in the industry. Collaborative filtering may include
collaborative filtering based on similar users (UserCF) and collaborative filtering based on
similar items (ItemCF). Because of the complexity of unstructured data, the recommender
system has many optimization details based on the basic algorithm. Such cases are numerous.
In 2011, Youtube discovered that the user clicks the video does not necessarily mean that
users have seen this video. Therefore, YouTube adds the consideration of video viewing time
to learn users’ viewing habits more accurately. Another example: Netflix will consider
whether it should recommend different content to two people from different regions and
cultures - even if they like science fiction movies. For another example, many multimedia
websites will provide the function of style tags and scoring mechanism, which are conducive
to organizing data and data mining.

4.5 Data Security and Privacy

Companies will not ignore the needs of users for data security and privacy. When we register
app or website users, we can often see the privacy policy-although few people will read it
carefully. But this represents an effort that people are making. Google and other websites use
strong encryption technology and confidentiality agreements to protect the storage and
transmission of data and prevent network attacks. If you are not satisfied with advertising
recommendations, many websites provide the function of canceling personalized
customization. At the same time, federal learning has been put forward as an important
technology to solve the privacy problem of recommender systems.

5 Solutions proposed by me

The solution I want to propose aims at the most important problem in the recommender
system: how to make the recommendation results more accurate. I think in the application
context of e-commerce shopping websites, artificial intelligence technology (such as an
expert system) can be used to optimize the recommendation results. If the recommender
system only focuses on the user behavior itself or the product itself, the recommendation
results may not be accurate enough. For example, in winter, if users buy a pair of snow boots,
they will not buy other snow boots. However, the recommendation algorithms of many ecommerce websites often ignore this point and recommend snow boots or other similar
products based on users’ search and purchase records.
In order to solve this problem, we can train the expert program in the field of clothing
matching in advance, grasp the current fashion, quarter, brand, and other clothing
professional knowledge, and recommend the matching sweaters, down jackets, and other
goods for users according to the snow boots they have purchased. In this way, other related
recommendations based on users’ existing purchase behavior can not only avoid
recommending popular goods but also give relative consideration to users’ portraits.
